# Full Day Deep Sea Fishing | Charleston, SC

If this is your first full-day deep sea fishing trip aboard the Wahoo, you may be surprised at the differences from in-shore fishing and even short day trips. A full day of deep sea fishing allows for a wider range of experiences and chances at even more exciting catches. There are a few things you should be aware of going in, to prepare to have your best day of fishing ever.![Deep Sea Fishing Charleston, SC](/blog-images/DJI_0051-300x225.jpg)

# Deep Sea Fishing is Physically Challenging

If you’re an “arm chair fisherman,” more prone to fishing from shore or from a bass boat than a well-appointed cruiser like the Wahoo, you may be in for a bit of a shock. The tackle and poles used in deep sea fishing are similar to the equipment used for inshore and freshwater fishing, but meatier. The poles have to be thicker and stronger, and the line heavier, to handle the shocks of a really big game fish taking the bait. The crew is on hand to help you bring in that big prize, but you’ll find that deep sea fishing is an exhilarating, physical experience.

# It Takes Time

This should go without saying, but in the excitement and anticipation of hooking that big grouper or Mahi Mahi tuna, it’s easy to overlook the time it takes to actually reach the depths that provide the biggest, most aggressive game fish.   Fortunately, the Wahoo’s comfortable facilities, satellite television and radio, and other features ensure that you’ll pass the time in comfort. Sit back, enjoy the ride, and you’ll be wetting your line and fighting with big fish before you know it.

# It’s Variable

Depending upon the season, the time of day, and the most current regulations, you can catch any number of species of deep-sea fish. Grouper, sailfish, triggerfish, black sea bass, wahoo, and tuna are all popular gamefish. An all-day deep sea fishing trip aboard the Wahoo gives you the most opportunities at various species and peak fishing times. You’ll be able to experience both trolling and bottom fishing, maximizing your chances at hooking large, aggressive, exciting fish.
