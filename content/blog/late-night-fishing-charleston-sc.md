Late night fishing offshore is a rewarding experience that is not to be missed. Especially during the long, hot days of summer, overnight fishing trips offer you two shots at the prime-times for catching big, hard-fighting fish. During the hottest months, the best shot at large fish like sailfish is [trolling](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Trolling_\(fishing\)) the 120-300 foot depths further out offshore. Mackerel may be found closer to shore, but if you’re going for the real fight, an overnight trip just can’t be beaten.

![Late Night Fishing Charleston SC](/blog-images/DJI_0048-300x225.jpg)

# Late Night Fish

Yellowfin tuna are another important late-summer species that offer a spectacular fight and fill the cooler. By summer’s end, the average yellowfin will weigh around 45 pounds. For anglers looking for a challenge, amberjack and barracuda are a little picky during the hottest days, but are amenable to live bait, especially around ship wrecks or reefs.

# Late Night Fishing – Charleston Fishing Charters

On an [overnight trip](/packages-and-prices/), we head out on our 50 foot well-appointed luxury yacht, the Wahoo. We set sail at noon. We’ll head offshore and troll for big game fish until the biting slows down. Once the action has calmed, we switch off to bottom fishing or go for swordfish overnight. After a night of fighting some of the biggest, strongest fish you’ve ever had on the end of a line, we’ll switch back to trolling for more big game fish or bottom fishing to round out the catch. We’ll head back in at 12PM the next day, with a cooler full of fish and memories to last a lifetime.

Late night fishing offers a unique experience and chances at landing some of the biggest game fish the ocean off Charleston, SC has to offer.
