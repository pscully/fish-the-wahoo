# Charleston SC and When to Catch Tuna, Wahoo, and Marlin

[![Catch Tuna, Wahoo, and Marlin](/blog-images/DSC_3051-300x178.jpg)Fishing in Charleston, SC](/) has become a favorite pastime, and when you know precisely what you’re aiming to catch, it’s important that you know which season will most likely grant your fishing wishes! There are certain months out of the year that works better for you to catch specific fish, and we’re going to help you by providing the Best Months of the Year to Catch Tuna, Wahoo, and Marlin in Charleston.

# When to Catch Tuna in Charleston, SC

Fishing for tuna in Charleston, SC is typically available throughout the year but from the middle of March through April, the waters tend to warm up as the [weather becomes hotter which helps stabilize the temperature](http://oceanworld.tamu.edu/resources/oceanography-book/oceansandclimate.htm). Fishing for tuna really starts to heat up during these months and can be an excellent time to catch tuna ranging from 15 to 70 lbs. During the latter part of April, you’ll see even more fish beginning to migrate into the South Carolina waters more consistently.

# When to Catch Wahoo in Charleston, SC

You can find more [wahoo](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wahoo) during the end of April more than any other time of year along with dolphins. As the month of May starts rolling around, you just never know what you’re going to catch! Wahoo has dominated the catch for May for years and as the middle of the month starts to approach you will be entering the prime season to get the best catch with light tackle.

# When to Catch Marlin in Charleston, SC

The best time to catch marlin in Charleston starts toward the middle to end of May when the marlin season begins. You’ll see even more marlin during the peak of the season in June all the way through the middle of July. It is common to see [white marlin](/2015/04/07/white-marlin-fishing/) during this time because this period offers the best catch of the year.
