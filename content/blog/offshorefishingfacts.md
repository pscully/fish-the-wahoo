Offshore fishing can be a great hobby for seasoned fishermen as well as first-timers. If you have years of experience or if you’re going on your first fishing charter with [Fish the Wahoo](/), there are some key facts you need to know.

# ![Experienced Deep Sean Captain - Charleston, SC](/blog-images/unnamed-4-300x200.jpg)Here are ten basic facts you should know about offshore fishing:

1.  **You can catch a variety of fish on your deep sea fishing trip.** Tuna, marlin, sailfish, wahoo, [mahi-mahi](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mahi-mahi), striped bass, haddock, barracuda, snapper, salmon, shark, blue fish, and many others are frequently caught on offshore fishing charters.
2.  **Offshore fishing is done in water at or near 100 feet deep.** To put this into perspective, if you’re deep sea fishing, your boat will not easily be seen from the shore. While deep sea fishing is done commercially as well, the term is usually used to refer to the recreational type.
3.  **There are several methods used when deep sea fishing.** The most common are drifting, popping, bottom-fishing, jigging, and trolling. Each method uses its own equipment, is used for various locations, and to catch specific species.
4.  **Equipment used for deep sea fishing must be corrosion resistant and heavy duty.** That way you can avoid damage from excessive pressure and salt water exposure.
5.  **You must be patient.** Just as you take your time when fishing from the shore, one of the best deep sea fishing facts is that you must be patient. It will take some time to get a bite.
6.  **For obvious reasons, deep sea fishing is more dangerous than fishing from shore.** Add weather, tides, and the various times of year, and the levels of danger vary as well. One deep sea fishing fact is to make sure you are prepared. Ensure your boat has the needed supplies in case of an emergency, and familiarize yourself with the proper emergency procedures.
7.  **Your captain knows best.** When in doubt, ask your captain. He or she may know some tips or tricks, or can tell you what errors you’re making that can help you to improve your chances. The captain doesn’t just drive the boat.
8.  **Since the early 1950s, less than 170 black and blue marlin weighing 1,000 pounds or more have been hooked.** The largest marlin was last caught in December 2010, weighing in at 1,011 pounds.
9.  **The United States has several regions where deep sea fishing is popular.** These include regions off the coasts of Connecticut, Maine, and Massachusetts, Florida, and South Carolina, Texas, and Alaska. In addition, many Americans travel to exotic locations such as the Bahamas, Mexico, and the Caribbean for deep sea fishing adventures.
10.  **Deep sea fishing trips can cost anywhere from a couple hundred dollars to several thousand,** depending on how many individuals attend, how long the trip lasts, and the size of the boat. As deep sea fishing adventures can be an investment, be sure to get recommendations from locals and past customers when booking your charter service.
