# How to Tell if a Fishing Charter is Operated by an Experienced Captain- Charleston, SC

![Experienced Deep Sea Captain - Charleston, SC](/blog-images/unnamed-4-300x200.jpg)Anyone going on a fishing charter will want to ensure their captain is experienced, qualified, and will give them an enjoyable experience. Since you’re going to be with them for your 4, 6, or 12 hour fishing trip, it is important that you feel safe on the boat with your captain. There are typically [five things](http://fishingcharters.com/blog/choosing-fish-captain/) that make or break an experienced captain. Here they are:

1.  Ask about how long the charter has been in business. Your [captain](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sea_captain) will be happy to answer any questions you have, including the longevity of their charter business. Be polite, but straightforward, and be sure to ask the captain directly, not the person in the marina office.
2.  Ask if the business is a full-time or part-time charter operation. Running a charter business can be incredibly expensive, so most professional charter fishermen have a side career that they focus on when fishing is not in season. Keep in mind that there is nothing wrong with this and it does not in any way impact a captain’s ability to catch fish or provide an outstanding experience for their customers.
3.  Ask about your captain’s fishing experience. An experienced captain would love to tell you about their fishing experience in the area. See if they are well versed in multiple fishing techniques and is knowledgeable about the best methods to target seasonal species.
4.  Ask about a typical day on the water. A really experienced captain should be able to give you a rundown of his daily routine. From the second they leave the dock to the second they get back, they should have a plan that is shaped around the current season.
5.  Ask about their tournament experience. It isn’t required for a captain to have participated in a tournament, but some do to gain experience and reputation. If your captain has completed a few tournaments, however, it’s a good sign that they have refined their techniques and tactics in a pressured environment. If they can perform well under pressure, it’s a good sign that they will also do a great job with you out on the water!
